The Market
The demand is proven. The opportunity is documented.
The Population

Israeli male population aged 25–54: approximately 1.73 million (Israel CBS, 2023). Extending to age 25+: approximately 2.68 million Israeli men.

The Clinical Reality

ED prevalence in Israeli men aged 25–55, based on a large-scale Israeli SHIM survey of 9,956 men: 25.2%. Broken down: 21.8% in men aged 25–39, rising to 33.7% in men aged 40–55. Applying these rates to the current population gives an estimated 473,000 men with ED aged 25–54 in Israel — between 930,000 and 1,030,000 Israeli men at 25+. ED prevalence is further elevated in men with diabetes and hypertension — both growing populations in Israel.

The Grey Market Reality
Israeli police have raided and seized multiple domestic Kamagra gel production laboratories. Locally manufactured counterfeit product exists and is in circulation.
November 2025: Israeli customs seized approximately 30,000 packs of suspected smuggled Viagra in a single air cargo shipment.
Israeli MOH and police have issued repeated public warnings about illegal ED product sales outside licensed pharmacies — dating back to 2010 and continuing to the present.
Ongoing Interpol operations targeting illicit medicine networks cite millions of units intercepted annually across global operations, with Israel identified as both a destination and transit market.
WhatsApp and Telegram are documented primary distribution channels for grey market performance products in Israel. Demand flows through digital channels, not physical retail.
The Stigma Driver

Israeli qualitative research on GP attitudes to sexual dysfunction confirms a consistent pattern: men avoid clinical conversations about performance, particularly at older ages. GPs report discomfort initiating these discussions. The result is a large population that is clinically eligible, financially willing, and structurally underserved by licensed channels. The grey market fills this gap not because it is better — but because it removes the conversation entirely.

The Digital Readiness

79% of Israelis aged 65+ used the internet in 2022 (CBS). WhatsApp penetration in Israel is among the highest globally. The infrastructure for a WhatsApp-native subscription service already exists in the hands of the target population.

The financial framing: Unit economics will be modelled once bundle composition is confirmed, pharmacy cost structure is established, and pricing is locked. These inputs are Phase 1 decisions. The market size is large enough and the demand documented enough to proceed to proof of concept without revenue projections. The numbers get built on real data, not assumptions.

Chapter 4
The Marketing Edge
The structural advantage no competitor can replicate
The Problem

Israeli MOH Rule 24/04 prohibits direct-to-consumer advertising of prescription medications. A pharmacy cannot run a Meta ad for Tadalafil. It cannot make outcome claims about performance or lifestyle. It cannot frame a subscription around a man’s identity and confidence.

Selling prescription medications alone is a low-margin, high-regulation, commodity business. Selling only non-prescription supplements lacks clinical authority. The grey market wins on experience while carrying enormous product safety risk and zero retention capability.

The Edge

PROOF will operate through a partnership with a licensed clinical entity. Combining a clinically supervised prescription protocol alongside a non-prescription bundle — inside a single patient relationship — is the only model that solves both problems simultaneously. This combination is what no pharmacy, no supplement brand, and no grey market operator can legally construct.

Protocol marketing under MOH Guideline 137: A licensed clinical partnership enables health education and awareness content about men’s performance framed around outcomes and lifestyle, not molecules. ‘Always ready. No timing required.’ is awareness content, not a drug advertisement.
SEO and content authority: A clinical platform publishing expert content on men’s performance builds genuine search authority that grey market operators structurally cannot claim. SEO compounds over time and costs nothing per lead once established.
Consultation-led acquisition: Traffic drives to a clinical consultation — not a product page. The patient enters a physician relationship. The prescription follows from that relationship.
Non-prescription bundle marketed freely: Non-prescription components can be advertised with considerably more freedom under Israeli nutritional supplement regulations. Under MOH Rule 24/04, prescription and non-prescription products cannot be advertised together — the marketing architecture keeps them separated.
Cross-sell through the clinical relationship: Performance subscribers introduced to the hair protocol. Hair subscribers introduced to performance. Only possible within a licensed clinical platform.
The Competitive Moat
The clinical partnership — establishing a founding relationship with licensed physicians who understand and commit to the platform model takes time and trust that cannot be replicated quickly.
The MSO architecture — the legal and financial structure that makes the combined prescription and non-prescription model work is non-trivial to build correctly.
Patient data and relationships — every subscriber who enters the PROOF platform becomes a longitudinal data asset and a cross-sell candidate with compounding value.
The WhatsApp-native subscription infrastructure — automation sequences, physician confirmation flow, renewal mechanics — represent operational IP that takes time to build and optimise.

Regulatory boundary note: This marketing architecture is consistent with Israeli regulatory frameworks as currently understood. Campaign copy, channel selection, and content classification require sign-off from Israeli regulatory counsel before launch.

Speed and discretion are the entire product

From the patient’s perspective, this is a WhatsApp conversation that ends with medication at his door the same day. Every second of friction is a lost sale. The regulatory minimum is made as invisible as possible. The experience signals luxury, not medicine.

Harvard Business Review research on 2.24 million leads: companies responding within one hour are 7x more likely to qualify a lead. 78% of buyers go with the first company that responds. After one hour, the lead is likely already talking to a competitor.

Legal name and ID collected securely within the WhatsApp conversation before the physician call is scheduled. This is the latest clinically and legally defensible moment for identity disclosure. 4

Synchronous Clinical Confirmation

The physician verifies identity, confirms the absence of contraindications, and establishes the physician-patient relationship required by MOH Circular 6/2019. Audio by default — no camera, no lighting, no dressing up. The patient experiences this as a brief confirmation before dispatch. Legally it is the synchronous interaction that makes every subsequent automated renewal fully compliant.

Under current Israeli MOH Circular 6/2019 as we understand it, the synchronous interaction is required for first prescriptions. Any operator currently bypassing this requirement is taking significant regulatory risk. The regulatory requirement is real. The experience of it is designed to be invisible.

Chapter 10
Open Questions
Six questions that need answers before Phase 1 builds

These are not for the current meeting — the Hair Blueprint agenda takes priority. Each question below is known, scoped, and will be resolved in the order it becomes relevant. None of them block the founding conversation. Together they define the Phase 1 build.

A
Bundle Composition

What non-prescription components do Dr. Eric and Dr. Asi Peretz clinically believe in for the performance protocol? The bundle is what separates PROOF from a commodity prescription service and what justifies a premium subscription price. Until bundle composition is agreed, pricing cannot be locked and the full marketing edge cannot be activated.

B
Physician Availability — The Speed Promise

For Phase 0 and Phase 1, physician availability for confirmation calls has been operationally agreed — this is a manageable volume at early stage. As patient volume scales beyond Phase 1, dedicated physician coverage with defined hours becomes a structural necessity. At that point, employed or contracted physicians with specific coverage commitments replace the early-stage arrangement. This transition is planned and expected — not reactive.

C
Same-Day Delivery — Operationally Real or Phase 1 Aspiration?

Same-day in the Tel Aviv metro is the product promise. Before it becomes a patient-facing commitment, PROOF confirms courier integration and pharmacy fulfilment proximity. If same-day is not achievable within 60 days of Phase 1 launch — next-day becomes the promise and the marketing adjusts accordingly.

D
Clinical Comfort — Daily Tadalafil as Optimisation Protocol

The physician confirms clinical comfort prescribing daily Tadalafil to a healthy man in his thirties or forties as a performance optimisation protocol — not as treatment for a diagnosed condition. This is a clinical framing question, not a regulatory one. The answer shapes the intake questionnaire and the physician’s language in the confirmation call.

E
UAE Regulatory Timeline

When are Dr. Eric and Dr. Asi Peretz’s UAE medical licences active? The performance line is the lead product for the Gulf launch — their regulatory readiness determines whether UAE is a Month 6 or Month 12 milestone.

F
Delivery Format Gap — Phase 2 Product Development

Gel and sublingual formats used by grey-market operators offer faster onset and a less clinical experience. This is a real product gap. Domestic compounding is not a viable path under Israeli MOH compounding regulation. The realistic Phase 2 paths are imported branded alternatives with differentiated delivery formats, or a formal product development and registration process. This is a question to raise with the clinical partner and with regulatory counsel at Phase 1 exit.
